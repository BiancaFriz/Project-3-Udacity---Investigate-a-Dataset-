Introduction to the analysis¶

On this oportunity the data that we will be exploring is the "No show appointment" data which collects information from 100k medical appointments in Brazil and is focused on the question of whether or not patients show up for their appointment.

These are the variables incluided with their definition:

PatientID: ID of the patient that solicited the appointment
AppointmentID: ID of the appointment
Gender: The gender (M or F) of the patient
ScheduledDay:The day that the patient set up their appointment.
AppointmentDay:The day the patient actually went to the appointment.
Age: Age of the patient
Neighbourhood: indicates the location of the hospital.
Scholarship: indicates whether or not the patient is enrolled in Brasilian welfare program "Bolsa Família".
Hipertension: 1 if the patient has hipertension, 0 if not
Diabetes: 1 if the patient has diabetes, 0 if not
Alcoholism: 1 if the patient has alcoholism, 0 if not
Handcap:Can be up to 4
SMS_received: 1 if the SMS was received, 0 if not
No-show: It says ‘No’ if the patient showed up to their appointment, and ‘Yes’ if they did not show up.

In this data analysis we will be answering the following questions:

1) What factors are important for us to know in order to predict if a patient will show up for their scheduled appointment?
2) Is the welfare a good or bad incentive for people to go to their appointment?
3) What are the illness that tend to miss more their appointment
4) Does sending an SMS helps people to show up to their appointment?

-> On the other hand, we will be making changes on the negative ages and create a new boolean column for "No-show" to get the correlation matrix and see the variables that have a bigger impact on showing up or not to the appointment.

On the first place, is important to talk about some of the limitations that this dataset has, which makes a barrier to make deeper conclusions like, for example, that there is missing a column that could include the neighbourhood where de patient lives and the km of distance it has to the location of the hospital. So it could show if the distance has a bigger impact in no showing to the appointment.

On the other hand, given that the sample is only of 100k it does not represent the behaviour of most people. Furthermore, because the average age of the data is 37, we could say that the dataset is quite young. Due to that, the illness present such as Diabetes, Hipertension are in a few amount of people from the sample. This limits, some further conclusions as to what illness may impact more in to show or not to the appointment and also, impacts the percentage of correlation with the "No-show" variable.

In spite of the lack of representation or other variables that could help to make a deeper analysis. The variables presented in the data were enough to answer some of the questions.

Firstly, because there is 65% of females in the dataset is consequent that they could be more females that did not show up to the appointment. This is true due to the "F, Yes" with 13.2% of the sample, which represents the females that did not showed to the appointment versus a 6,99% represented by man.

In adition, the analysis shows that the variables with more correlation with showing or not to the appointment, represented by "Noshow_2" are the AppointmentID, SMS_received and the Age.


Secondly, the answer if the welfare is a good or bad incentive for people to go to their appointment, is that it is a good incentive because most of the people that have this benefit did show up to their appointment, represented by the 76% of the people that has this welfare. However, the bigger group of people that did go to the appointment, were people that did not have the welfare, being the 72,9% of the sample.

At the same time, this could also mean, that people that do not have scholarship find it more difficult to have an appointment which can implicate that they are more responsible so they go on their scheduled day.

On the other side, out of Hipertension, Diabetes and Alcoholism. Hipertension with a 3,4% is the illness that tends to miss more apointments, which could also be because is the one that has more presence between people from the dataset.

Lastly, out of the 35.482 people that had SMS_received, 72% did go to the appointment, which means that it is an effective way to remind people of their scheduled appointment. Finally, as the exploration of the dataset shows, it would be interesting if the spectrum of people that received the SMS could increase so that more people would be reminded of their appointment day.
